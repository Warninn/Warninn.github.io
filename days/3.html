HTML defines six levels of headings, and these heading elements are H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, and H6. The H1 element is the highest or most important level, and the H6 element is the least important.

These different heading levels help to communicate the organization and hierarchy of the content on a page. For example, headings with an equal or higher ranking indicate the start of a new section, and headings with a lower rank indicate the start of a new subsection that is part of a higher-ranked section.
